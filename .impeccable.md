## Design Context

### Users
Facility staff (coaches) operate the tablet during live sessions — they need instant readability under low-light gym conditions. Participants see the workout display mirrored on large TVs while actively training — their attention is split, so data must read at a glance from across the room. The emotional peak of the experience is the live session: real-time intensity zones, milestones, rankings. The interface is a performance instrument, not a dashboard.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Powerful. Human. Relentless.

Voice: short and rhythmic ("Train with data." / "Push further." / "You've got more in you."). Direct, science-backed, never arrogant. Uses "we" and "us" to build community. No fluff, no jargon.

Tagline: **Push Smarter. Live Better.**

Emotional goals: confidence during the session, pride in progress, belonging to something bigger.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone:** Bold, dark, cinematic. Think Apple Fitness+ meets Barry's Bootcamp — premium tech precision with raw gym energy. Clean information hierarchy at large scale, but with moments of high visual impact (zone changes, milestones, rankings).

**Primary palette (brand manual):**
- Black: `#111112` / `#000000`
- Kardia Red: `#eb282c` (digital) / `#D84134` (print)
- Off-white: `#F4F4F4`
- Secondary accent: `#a6ff00` (neon lime — used sparingly)

**Dark mode first.** Always. The facility app runs on black (`#000`), mirrored to TVs in lit gyms.

**References:** Beat 81, Barry's Bootcamp, Apple Fitness+, Freeletics, Adidas Training, Nike Training Club. What we want from them: clean information density, bold typography, dynamic motion, premium feel.

**Anti-references:** Generic fitness apps (MyFitnessPal, generic gym SaaS). Never clinical, corporate, soft, rounded, or dated. Not another red-and-black template. Must be memorable.

**Graphic language from brand manual:**
- The K isotipo is used as a bold graphic element — cropped, oversized, at cut
- Strong geometry, asymmetric layouts, red as a dominant color block
- Text and logos always left-aligned
- **No rounded corners on buttons and graphic resources** (brand rule)
- No opacity on logos or text
- Only three brand colors — never outside the palette

**Typography:** Archivo (digital) / Neue Haas Grotesk Display Pro (brand print). In digital: Archivo 400–700. For display/impact numbers: weight 700–800, tight letter-spacing. Short, punchy copy.

**Animation language:** Game-like energy. Overshoot and settle. Scale with spring physics. Flash sweeps on zone changes. Cinematic milestone celebrations. Nothing subtle — this is a performance arena.

**Photography style:** Community, training, joy. Warm red tones. Real people working hard together, smiling.

### Design Principles

1. **Legibility at distance is non-negotiable.** Cards are read from across the room on a TV. Large numbers, high contrast, zero ambiguity. If it needs squinting, it fails.

2. **Energy follows data.** The interface should feel alive. Zone changes, milestones, and rankings must feel like moments — not just state changes. Use animation to make progress tangible and exciting.

3. **Bold geometry over decoration.** Use strong shapes, color blocks, and the K mark as graphic elements. Don't add flourishes — remove everything that doesn't earn its place.

4. **Dark, never dim.** Deep blacks and rich contrast. Never grey washes or low-opacity treatments. Colors should feel saturated and confident, not muted or clinical.

5. **Community over individual.** The grid of 6 workout cards is a team scoreboard, not a personal tracker. Design decisions should reinforce togetherness and shared effort — everyone visible, everyone counted.
